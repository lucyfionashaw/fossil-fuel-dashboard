Import Dependency Trends (2000–2023)

How import dependency has shifted over two decades. US became a net energy exporter; China's oil and gas dependency grew sharply.

Oil Import Dependency Over Time

Gas Import Dependency Over Time

Key shifts: The US shale revolution made the US a net petroleum exporter by 2020 and net energy exporter by 2019. Meanwhile, China's oil import dependency rose from 31% (2000) to 72% (2023), and gas imports grew from near zero to 40%. The EU's gas dependency was stable at ~80% but the 2022 Russia-Ukraine crisis forced rapid supplier diversification away from Russian pipeline gas (40% to ~15% by 2023).
