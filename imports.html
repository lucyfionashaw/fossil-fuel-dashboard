Methodology & Sources

Import dependency is calculated as net imports (imports minus exports) divided by total primary energy consumption for each commodity. A negative figure indicates the country is a net exporter. Data is for 2023 unless otherwise noted.

Energy Security Risk Index is a composite metric: import dependency share (40% weight), Herfindahl-Hirschman Index of supplier concentration (30% weight), and strategic/commercial stockpile cover in days of consumption (30% weight, inverted so more days = lower risk). Scores normalized to 0–100 scale.

Coal: Production and trade data from IEA Coal Information 2024, Energy Institute Statistical Review 2024 (74th ed.), and national statistical offices (NBS China, Ministry of Coal India, EIA for US). Thermal and metallurgical coal combined unless noted.

Oil: Crude oil + condensate + NGLs. Production from EIA International Energy Statistics and OPEC ASB 2024. Consumption from IEA Oil Information 2024. US became net petroleum exporter in 2020 (EIA Monthly Energy Review).

Gas: Marketed production and consumption from Energy Institute SR 2024 and IEA Gas Information 2024. LNG vs pipeline split from GIIGNL Annual Report 2024 and IGU World LNG Report 2024.

Supply diversification: Bilateral trade flows from UN Comtrade (HS 2701 coal, 2709 crude oil), IEA gas trade data, and GIIGNL for LNG. HHI calculated as sum of squared market shares of suppliers.
