Types of Coal Taxation

Countries tax coal through multiple overlapping instruments. This section captures the total fiscal burden — royalties, carbon pricing, resource levies, SOE dividends, and corporate income tax.

Production Royalties

Ad valorem or per-tonne charges on coal extracted. The most common coal-specific fiscal instrument.

Australia, India, Indonesia, USA, Colombia, South Africa

Carbon Pricing

ETS or carbon tax applied to CO2 from coal combustion. Creates implicit per-tonne cost on coal.

EU ETS, UK Carbon Price Support, South Africa carbon tax

Resource / Cess Taxes

Dedicated levies on coal production or consumption, often earmarked. Separate from royalties.

India GST compensation cess, China resource tax, DMF/NMET

SOE Dividends & Income Tax

Government revenue from owning coal assets (dividends, profit transfers) and corporate income tax on coal companies.

Coal India, China Shenhua/China Energy, Eskom

How Governments Capture Coal Revenue

Revenue channels vary by country. Royalties dominate in Australia and Colombia; India collects through a complex mix of royalties, cess, GST, and SOE dividends; Indonesia uses a production sharing / PNBP system.

Key Data Gaps

China: The world's largest coal producer (~4.7 Bt in 2023) publishes no coal-specific fiscal data. Resource tax revenue (all minerals combined) was CNY 307 billion (~$43B) in 2023, but the coal share is not disaggregated. Academic estimates suggest ~$42-48B in total coal-related taxes (including VAT, CIT, resource tax, and local surcharges), which would make China by far the largest collector of coal revenue globally — but this cannot be verified from official sources.
Russia: Fiscal data restricted since 2022. Kuzbass region (producing ~60% of Russian coal) reported NDPI of 17.7B rubles (~$220M) in 2023, suggesting national coal NDPI of ~$350-400M. Total coal fiscal contribution including CIT likely $1.2-2.5B but unverifiable.
Kazakhstan & Mongolia: Both are significant coal producers but EITI reports are delayed (Kazakhstan suspended, Mongolia's 18th report recently published). Coal-specific revenue is not separately reported in available English-language sources.

Methodology & Sources

Revenue Estimation

  • Reported revenue: Where governments publish coal royalty/tax revenue figures (e.g., Australian state treasury data, US ONRR, India ministry reports), these are used directly and tagged REPORTED.
  • Calculated estimates: Where only statutory rates and production data are available, revenue is estimated as: Production (Mt) × Average price ($/t) × Rate (%). These are tagged CALC with the formula shown.
  • Carbon pricing: Coal's share of ETS/carbon tax revenue is estimated from coal's share of covered emissions × total scheme revenue. This is necessarily approximate.
  • All figures converted to USD at 2023 average exchange rates (1 AUD = 0.66 USD; 1 INR = 0.012 USD; 1 EUR = 1.08 USD; 1 ZAR = 0.055 USD).

Scope

  • Included: Production royalties, carbon pricing (ETS/carbon tax), resource/cess taxes, severance taxes, export duties, SOE dividends, and corporate income tax from coal companies.
  • Excluded: General import duties on coal, general VAT/GST (except India's coal-specific GST compensation cess), and indirect economic benefits. Downstream taxes (VAT on coal sales, carbon taxes on coal consumption) are excluded unless bundled in source data (as in India's GST + cess).

Country Notes

Indonesia: PNBP (non-tax state revenue) from the mineral and coal sub-sector via ESDM. Coal's share (~70% of minerba PNBP) estimated from official statements. Corporate income tax from coal companies collected separately by DG Tax and not included.

Australia: State-level royalties from Queensland Treasury Budget Papers (FY23-24 actual: A$10.52B) and NSW Revenue (FY24-25 estimate: A$2.95B). Federal CIT from coal companies not separately reported by ATO. USD conversion at average AUD/USD rates.

India: Coal India Ltd total exchequer contribution (Rs 60,198 crore in FY23-24) from Ministry of Coal Annual Report. Includes royalties (14% ad valorem to states), GST (5% + Rs 400/t cess), CIT, dividends, DMF, and NMET. Excludes private coal companies and Singareni Collieries.

South Africa: Mineral royalties from DMRE Mineral Economics Bulletin. Coal was 33.7% of total mineral royalties in FY22-23. Corporate income tax from coal companies not separately reported by SARS.

Colombia: Mining royalties from ANM and EITI Colombia. Coal is ~85% of mining royalties. 10% royalty rate for open-pit, 5% underground (above 3Mt/yr production).

USA: Federal coal royalties from ONRR (12.5% surface, 8% underground). Estimated from historical trend and production data. State severance taxes (Wyoming, West Virginia, etc.) not included in the verified revenue total but shown in the detailed breakdown.

Poland: Net subsidy position documented by CAN Europe, Instrat, and Polish government budget papers. Coal mines operate at losses covered by government support.
