Methodology & Sources

Scope: Government revenue from fossil fuel extraction and production, including royalties, corporate income tax on upstream/midstream operations, production sharing agreements, SOE/NOC dividends and profit transfers, export duties, and windfall/resource rent taxes. Downstream consumption taxes (VAT, fuel excise) excluded.

Oil & gas: NOC financials from annual reports (Saudi Aramco, ADNOC, QatarEnergy, Rosneft, Gazprom, Petrobras, ONGC, Petronas, Sonatrach, NNPC). Fiscal data from IMF Article IV reports, EITI country reports, and NRGI Resource Governance Index. Norway: Norwegian Petroleum Directorate net cash flow + SDFI. Russia: IMF/Ministry of Finance budget data (oil & gas revenues as % of federal budget).

Coal: Australia royalties from Queensland Treasury (QTRSS), NSW Resources Regulator. Indonesia PNBP from ESDM/MoF. India: Coal India annual report (dividends, CIT, royalties to states). USA: ONRR for federal coal royalties. China: fiscal data fragmented across provincial finance bureaus.

Trend data: IMF Fiscal Monitor, World Bank commodity price data, EITI Summary Data. 2022 peak reflects post-Ukraine energy price spike. 2023 normalization as prices retreated from highs.

Caveats: SOE revenue figures include substantial internal costs; net government take is lower than gross revenue. Many countries (especially non-EITI members) have limited fiscal transparency. Coal revenue data particularly opaque for China and Russia domestic operations. All figures in nominal USD.
