Methodology & Sources

Coal: Country-level production from IEA Coal 2025, NBS (China), Ministry of Coal (India), ESDM (Indonesia), EIA (USA). Prices use country-specific benchmarks where available (Qinhuangdao, Coal India notified + all statutory levies, Newcastle FOB, Richards Bay FOB, HBA, PLV HCC). Domestic prices for ~82% of coal consumed domestically are estimated. Total market value range of $500B–$900B reflects deep uncertainty in domestic pricing. India: CIL blended all-in price ~$30/t includes royalty 14%, DMF 4.2%, NMET 0.28%, GST 5%, and GST Compensation Cess Rs400/t.

Oil: Production from EIA (crude + condensate). Prices mapped to country-specific benchmarks for 80 countries (WTI, Urals, WCS, Maya, Murban, Arab Light OSPs, OPEC basket grades). Country-specific pricing reduces total by ~$190B (7.7%) vs flat Brent, driven by Russia sanctions discount (-$160B), Canada WCS (-$31B), Iran (-$17B).

Gas: Production from Energy Institute Statistical Review 2025. Market value uses IGU Wholesale Gas Price Survey 2025 regional weighted averages. ~31% of gas consumed at <$2/MMBtu (subsidized). If all gas were at market prices, value would be ~$1.2T.

Commodity Comparison: Values from EIA/IEA (fossil fuels), BNEF/IEA World Energy Investment 2024 (clean energy), World Steel Association/Platts (iron ore), ICSG (copper), World Gold Council (gold), IAI/LME (aluminum), USGS/Adamas (rare earths), WNA/UxC (uranium). All 2023–2024 data.

Transport: Coal freight from IEA Coal 2025, S&P Global Platts assessments. Oil tanker rates from EIA, Clarksons Research. LNG chain from Oxford Energy, Timera Energy, CSIS. Pipeline tariffs from FERC, ARTC, Transnet.
