Why these numbers are hard to pin down: Global fossil fuel employment estimates vary widely by source. ILO and national statistics use different definitions (direct vs. indirect, full-time equivalent vs. headcount). Coal mining in China and India alone accounts for over half of all fossil fuel jobs globally, but official figures are often lagged or revised. Our estimates synthesize ILO, IRENA, national statistical offices, and industry body data for 2022-2023.

Fossil Fuels vs Renewable Energy: Global Employment

Renewable energy jobs have overtaken fossil fuel extraction employment and are growing faster. IRENA estimates 16.2 million renewable energy jobs in 2023, up from 7.3 million in 2012.

Coal Mining: Top Countries by Employment

China dominates with ~4 million coal miners — more than half the global total. Numbers for China reflect registered coal mine workers; informal/contract labor adds further.

Oil & Gas: Top Countries by Employment

Oil & gas employment is more evenly distributed, reflecting diverse upstream, midstream, and downstream operations across many producing countries.

Employment by Supply Chain Stage

Fossil fuel jobs span mining/extraction, transport and logistics, and processing/refining. Coal is uniquely labor-intensive at the extraction stage.

Data note: Power generation employment is excluded as reliable per-commodity figures are not available. Many power plants use multiple fuel types, making attribution difficult.

Labor Productivity: Tonnes Produced per Worker

Productivity varies enormously by mining method and mechanization. Australian open-cut operations produce 10-15x more tonnes per worker than Indian underground mines.

Methodology & Sources

Coal employment: China figure (~4M) based on National Bureau of Statistics and China Energy Statistical Yearbook for registered coal mine workers (2022). India (~500K-1M range) from Coal India Ltd annual reports (233K direct CIL employees, plus SCCL, captive mines, and contract labor). Indonesia (~250K) from BPS / ESDM. US (42K) from BLS Quarterly Census of Employment & Wages 2023. EU countries from Eurostat SBS.

Oil & Gas employment: Country-level data from ILO ILOSTAT database (ISIC Rev.4 codes 06, 09.1, 19, 49.5), supplemented by national statistics: US BLS (150K direct O&G extraction), Russia Rosstat, Saudi Arabia GASTAT, Brazil IBGE/ANP. Estimates include upstream extraction; midstream and refining add approximately 40-60% more jobs.

Renewables: IRENA & ILO "Renewable Energy and Jobs: Annual Review 2024" (data for 2023). Global total 16.2 million jobs. Solar PV is the largest employer (7.2M), followed by biofuels (2.7M), hydropower (2.3M), and wind (1.5M).

Caveats: Direct employment only (indirect/induced multiplier effects excluded, typically 2-4x for coal, 3-6x for oil & gas). Informal and contract labor in developing countries is significantly under-counted. Numbers rounded to nearest thousand.
