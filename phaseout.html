Retirements dwarfed by new builds
Coal capacity has doubled in 25 years, but the geography has shifted dramatically. In 2000, the Americas and Europe held 59% of global coal capacity. By 2025, their share has fallen to just 16%, replaced almost entirely by China (+HK), whose fleet grew 6x from 206 GW to 1,247 GW. Retirements are consistently dwarfed by additions at a ratio of approximately 1:3. Data from Global Energy Monitor (January 2026) and Ember annual electricity data.

Methodology & Sources

Coal capacity data: Operating, under-construction, planned/announced, and retired coal power plant capacity from the Global Energy Monitor (GEM) Global Coal Plant Tracker, January 2026 update. Capacity figures are in gross nameplate capacity (GW). Regional breakdown follows GEM methodology: China+HK, India, Rest of Asia, Americas, Europe, Africa.

Utilisation: Calculated as generation in year (Ember annual data) divided by average installed capacity (GEM). CREA data used for 2025 generation estimates for China and India.

Phaseout commitments: A country is classified as "committed" if its national government has made a public statement, enacted legislation, or signed an international agreement (e.g., PPCA) specifying a date to end unabated coal power generation. Conditional commitments (e.g., subject to international finance) are noted.

PPCA membership: The Powering Past Coal Alliance is a coalition of national and sub-national governments, businesses, and organisations committed to phasing out unabated coal power. As of 2024, the PPCA has 56 national government members. However, no major coal power country (China, India, USA, Indonesia) is a member.

Generation data: Regional coal electricity generation from Ember Global Electricity Review 2025 and the Energy Institute Statistical Review 2025. Regions: China+HK, India, Rest of Asia, Americas, Europe, Africa.
