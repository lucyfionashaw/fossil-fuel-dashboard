The cost crossover has happened. In most markets globally, new solar and wind are now cheaper than new coal power plants on an unsubsidised LCOE basis. The True Cost of Coal Electricity

Adding carbon costs ($50–200/tCO2) and health/air pollution costs reveals coal's real price. Coal emits ~0.9 tCO2/MWh on average.

Hidden costs of coal power: A typical coal plant emitting ~0.9 tCO2/MWh incurs additional costs of $45/MWh at $50/tCO2 carbon price, plus $20–40/MWh in health/air pollution damages (WHO/Lancet). This more than doubles the apparent LCOE, bringing the true cost to $130–250/MWh.

Annual Fossil Fuel Electricity Subsidies ($B)

Global fossil fuel subsidies for electricity: ~$180B/year (IEA 2024). The IMF estimates implicit subsidies (including unpaid externalities) at $1.3 trillion for coal power alone. Without these subsidies, coal electricity would be uncompetitive in nearly all markets.

Methodology & Sources

LCOE data: Lazard Levelised Cost of Energy Analysis v17 (April 2024), BNEF 2H 2024 LCOE Update, IRENA Renewable Power Generation Costs in 2023 (June 2024). Country-level data cross-referenced with IEA World Energy Outlook 2024 and national energy regulator publications. All figures are unsubsidised, new-build LCOE in 2024 USD.

Carbon costs: Social cost of carbon at $75/tCO2 (US EPA central estimate, Nov 2023 update). Sensitivity shown at $50/tCO2 and $200/tCO2. Coal emission factor: 0.9 tCO2/MWh (IPCC AR6 median for hard coal). Gas: 0.37 tCO2/MWh.

Health & air pollution: Based on WHO/Lancet Commission on Pollution and Health (2022 update), OECD Cost of Air Pollution (2024), and Myllyvirta et al. (2024). Country-specific health damage estimates vary by population density, existing pollution levels, and healthcare system costs. Range: $8–65/MWh.

Subsidies: IEA Fossil Fuel Subsidies Database (2024 update), IMF "Still Not Getting Energy Prices Right" (2023). Explicit subsidies (direct transfers) + implicit subsidies (unpriced externalities). Coal electricity subsidy figures include both supply-side (producer) and demand-side (consumer) subsidies.

Historical trends: IRENA Renewable Power Generation Costs time series (2010–2023), extended to 2024 with Lazard/BNEF data. Coal and gas historical from IEA World Energy Outlook retrospectives.

  • LCOE = (Capital costs + Fixed O&M + Variable O&M + Fuel costs) / Lifetime electricity output, discounted at WACC
  • Does not include integration costs (storage, grid) for variable renewables, or decommissioning costs for coal/nuclear
  • Country LCOE ranges reflect differences in resource quality, cost of capital, labour costs, and fuel prices
