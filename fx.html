Methodology & Sources

Export values: Based on UN Comtrade 2023 data (HS codes 2701-2704 for coal, 2709-2710 for crude oil and petroleum products, 2711 for natural gas incl. LNG). Cross-referenced with national customs data and Energy Institute Statistical Review 2024 (73rd edition) trade flow tables.

Coal exports: Volume data from IEA Coal 2024 and national sources. Australia values include significant met coal premium ($250-350/t vs $100-140/t thermal). Indonesia volumes adjusted for moisture content differences between Indonesian sub-bituminous and international grades.

Oil exports: Crude and condensate export volumes from OPEC Annual Statistical Bulletin 2024 and EIA International Energy Statistics. Values use country-specific benchmark prices (Brent, WTI, Urals, Dubai/Oman, etc.) rather than uniform Brent pricing. 2023 average Brent: $82.49/bbl.

Gas exports: Pipeline and LNG volumes from IGU World LNG Report 2024 and Energy Institute SR. LNG valued at destination market prices (JKM for Asia, TTF for Europe, Henry Hub + liquefaction for US exports). Pipeline gas valued at bilateral contract prices where available, regional benchmarks otherwise.

Dependency ratios: Fossil fuel exports as % of total merchandise exports. Total merchandise export data from WTO Statistics Database and World Bank WITS. Some ratios may differ from IMF/World Bank figures due to different treatment of re-exports and product classifications.

Import substitution: Domestic production valued at international benchmark prices to estimate "avoided import cost." Actual domestic prices are often lower (regulated markets in India, China, etc.), so this represents an upper bound on FX savings.
